## üéØ Program At A Glance

### Structure
- **Duration:** 4 terms (24-36 months depending on pace)
- **Total Credits:** 36
- **Format:** 8-week courses (vs. traditional 16-week)
- **Delivery:** Asynchronous-first + weekly synchronous studio sessions
- **Start:** Fall 2026

### Core Courses (16 credits)
1. **BADM 554** - Data Systems & Engineering (4 cr)
2. **BDI 513** - Data Storytelling (4 cr, split 2+2)
3. **FIN 550** - Analytics for Business (4 cr)
4. **BADM 557** - Business Intelligence with AI (4 cr)

### Advanced Courses (8 credits)
- **BADM 558** - Big Data Infrastructure (4 cr)
- **BADM 576** - Data Science & Machine Learning (4 cr)

### Electives & Capstone (12 credits)
- **Generative AI for Analytics** (2 cr) ‚Äî NEW
- **Specialization Courses** (4 cr) ‚Äî rotate by student interest
- **Capstone/Practicum** (4 cr) ‚Äî portfolio + new project

### Key Features
‚úÖ **AI-First:** Every course integrates AI tools (Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot)
‚úÖ **Project-Based:** 2-3 major projects per course, no traditional exams
‚úÖ **Python + Jupyter:** Universal computational backbone
‚úÖ **Studio Sessions:** Weekly live project-focused sessions with faculty
‚úÖ **GitHub Integration:** All student code on GitHub (version control habit)
‚úÖ **Stackable Pathways:**
   - Core Analytics Certificate (12 mo, 12 cr)
   - AI Analytics Certificate (18 mo, 18 cr)
   - Full Master's (24-36 mo, 36 cr)

## ‚ùì FAQs

**Q: Can students do this part-time?**
A: Yes. 8-week courses allow taking 1-2 courses per term while working. Most students take 2 courses/term (12 credits/year) ‚Üí 3-year path. Some accelerate to 4 courses/term ‚Üí 24-month path.

**Q: What if I miss a studio session?**
A: No penalty. All sessions recorded + uploaded within 24 hours. Async-first philosophy means you can catch up on your schedule.

**Q: Is AI tool use (Claude, ChatGPT) encouraged?**
A: Yes. We teach responsible AI usage. Students learn when to use AI vs. do manual work. AI is integrated into every course as a productivity tool.

**Q: Will there be real-time exams?**
A: No. All assessment is project-based. Projects demonstrate competency better than exams. No proctored exams.

**Q: How much time should I expect to spend per week?**
A: 15-20 hours/week on average. Includes: lectures (4-5 hrs), projects (6-8 hrs), studio sessions (1.5-2 hrs), readings/assignments (3-4 hrs). Intensive but doable for working professionals.

**Q: Will I get internships or job placement help?**
A: Yes. Dedicated career services staff: resume review, interview coaching, job board, employer recruiting events, alumni networking.

**Q: What's the capstone?**
A: Two-part capstone in Fall 4:
- Part 1 (Weeks 1-2): Portfolio curation + GitHub portfolio assembly
- Part 2 (Weeks 3-8): New individual project (real client or independent research with AI assist)

**Q: Are there scholarships?**
A: Yes. Need-based aid + merit scholarships available. Goal: 20% cheaper than peer programs. Active recruitment of underrepresented groups.
