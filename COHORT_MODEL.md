# MSBAi Cohort Model: Student Experience & Synchronous Touchpoints

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 12, 2026
**Status:** Ready for implementation

---

## Document Purpose

This document defines how MSBAi creates community, engagement, and support for online students despite asynchronous delivery. It covers:

1. **Cohort Structure:** Size, enrollment timing, diversity goals
2. **Synchronous Sessions:** Three distinct types (Studio, Conversation, Office Hours)
3. **Community Building:** Discord, peer learning, alumni networks
4. **Student Support:** Mentoring, career services, mental health resources
5. **Global Time Zones:** How we accommodate global students
6. **Onboarding & Offboarding:** First-day experience to graduation celebration

---

## Part 1: Cohort Structure

### Cohort Size & Composition

**Target Cohort Size:** 50-75 students per Fall intake

**Rationale:**
- Small enough for community (everyone knows each other)
- Large enough to sustain peer learning + discussions
- Manageable for 5-6 faculty + 3-5 TAs
- Economically viable (balance cost vs. revenue)

**Diversity Goals:**
- 40%+ underrepresented groups (women, first-gen, minorities, non-traditional)
- 30%+ international students (global time zones)
- 50%+ career switchers or first-time graduate students
- Mix of career stages (early-career, mid-career, career-pivoters)

**Enrollment Targets by Pathway:**
- Core Analytics Certificate: 10-15 students
- AI Analytics Certificate: 20-25 students
- Full Master's Degree: 25-35 students

### Cohort Identification & Continuity

**Cohort Naming:** "MSBAi Class of 2027-Fall1", "MSBAi Class of 2027-Spring1", etc.

**Alumni Integration:** Maintain multi-cohort community:
- Monthly "All-Alumni" meetups (Cohort 1, 2, 3, etc. together)
- Peer mentoring (advanced cohorts mentor new students)
- Industry speaker series (alumni bring companies)
- Job board exclusively for MSBAi graduates

## Part 2: Synchronous Session Types

### Session 1: Project Studio (90 minutes, weekly)

**Purpose:** Hands-on project work with live instructor guidance, peer collaboration, AI-augmented analysis demos.

**Format:**
- **First 30 min:** Live coding walkthrough of project challenge (instructor demos approach)
- **Next 45 min:** Students work on projects (breakout rooms for pair programming)
- **Last 15 min:** Debrief + Q&A + preview next week

**Instructor Role:**
- Share screen + live-code a solution approach
- Demonstrate AI tools (Claude for analysis, ChatGPT for code generation)
- Monitor breakout rooms, answer questions
- Share best practices + common pitfalls

**Attendance:**
- Optional but strongly encouraged (70%+ target)
- All sessions recorded + uploaded within 24 hours
- Asynchronous alternative: Pre-recorded walkthroughs + async Q&A on forum

**Scheduling:**
- Same time every week (TBD: Tue 10am UTC, Thu 6pm UTC, Sat 2pm UTC depending on cohort timezone)
- Rotate time slots after midterm (accommodate students across zones)
- Record all sessions for async access

**Tools:**
- Zoom (video conferencing)
- Breakout rooms (pair programming)
- Shared Jupyter notebooks (live coding demo)
- GitHub (share solution code during session)
- Discord (chat during studio for quick questions)

**Example Schedule:**

| Time | Course | Instructor |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Tue 10:00-11:30 UTC | BADM 554 + BDI 513 | Prof. [Name] |
| Wed 18:00-19:30 UTC | FIN 550 + BADM 557 | Prof. [Name] |
| Thu 14:00-15:30 UTC | BADM 558 + BADM 576 | Prof. [Name] |
| Sat 14:00-15:30 UTC | Rotating (catch-up) | TA [Name] |

### Session 2: Analytics Conversation (60 minutes, bi-weekly)

**Purpose:** High-engagement discussions on current topics, guest speakers, case study analysis, emerging trends in data/AI/analytics.

**Format Options:**

**Format A: Guest Speaker (Every Other Week)**
- Industry practitioner talks about real project (30 min)
- Student Q&A (20 min)
- Discussion: How could you approach this problem? (10 min)

**Format B: Case Study Discussion (Every Other Week)**
- Assign case 1 week in advance
- Breakout rooms: 4-5 students per room discuss approach
- Share group answers (30 min of presentations)
- Instructor synthesis (20 min)
- Vote on best approach

**Format C: Emerging Topics**
- Current events in data/AI (recent news, tech announcements)
- Students analyze impact + implications
- Debate: Should we use this technology?

**Guest Speaker Sources:**
- MSBAi alumni (back to mentor + share experience)
- Industry practitioners (finance, healthcare, tech companies)
- AI/ML researchers (academia or corporate R&D)
- Thought leaders (Data Science Council, local business community)

**Scheduling:**
- Bi-weekly alternating with Project Studio
- Consistent day/time (e.g., Fri 2:00pm UTC)
- Recorded for async access (live Q&A only)

**Sample Analytics Conversations (Year 1):**

| Date | Topic | Speaker |
|------|-------|---------|
| Sep 2026 | Real ML project in finance | JPMorgan quant |
| Oct 2026 | Building data teams | Former Google Analytics Lead |
| Nov 2026 | Ethics in ML | AI researcher from local university |
| Dec 2026 | AI-augmented analytics workflow | MSBAi Cohort 1 graduate |
| Jan 2027 | Trending in data engineering | Data engineer from startup |
| Feb 2027 | Healthcare data challenges | Hospital CIO |

### Session 3: Office Hours (30-60 minutes, weekly + drop-in)

**Purpose:** 1-on-1 or small-group mentoring, debugging, career advice, personal check-ins.

**Format:**
- **Recurring office hours:** Same time every week (10-15 min per student)
- **Drop-in hours:** Dedicated time for ad-hoc questions
- **Slack/Discord:** Asynchronous Q&A between sessions

**Instructor Responsibilities:**
- Code review + debugging help
- Conceptual questions ("I don't understand logistic regression")
- Project strategy ("Am I on the right track?")
- Career conversations ("Should I pivot to data engineering?")
- Mental health check-ins ("How are you coping with course load?")

**TA Responsibilities:**
- Initial triage (route to instructor if needed)
- Troubleshooting technical setup (AWS, Jupyter, GitHub)
- DataCamp/course platform issues
- Peer mentoring (senior TA helps junior students)

**Scheduling Options:**
- **Async-first model:** Slack/Discord + scheduled office hours
- **Recurring slots:** Instructors hold 2-3 hours office hours each week
- **Student sign-ups:** Book 15-30 min slots on faculty calendar
- **Cohort model:** All instructors available same time (students can pick who helps)

**Sample Weekly Office Hours Schedule:**
- Mon 3:00-4:00pm UTC: Prof A (BADM 554, BADM 557 questions)
- Wed 8:00-9:00pm UTC: Prof B (BDI 513, FIN 550 questions)
- Thu 3:00-4:00pm UTC: Prof C (BADM 558, BADM 576 questions)
- Fri 1:00-2:00pm UTC: All instructors available (open drop-in)

## Part 3: Community Building Strategies

### Discord Server (Primary Hub)

**Channels:**
- **#announcements:** Course updates, technical issues, university news
- **#general:** Off-topic, social, memes, random discussions
- **#intros:** New students introduce themselves (first post requirement)
- **#projects:** Share project work, ask for code review, celebrate completions
- **#algorithms:** Algorithm + data structure questions (peer help)
- **#ai-tools:** Discuss ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, AI workflows
- **#career:** Job postings, resume review, interview prep
- **#tech-setup:** Troubleshooting (GitHub, AWS, Python, Jupyter)
- **#cohort-events:** Organize meetups, game nights, study groups
- **#wins:** Celebrate successes (job offers, completed projects, big aha moments)

**Community Managers:**
- Rotate 1-2 student moderators (offer leadership role)
- 1 staff member (program coordinator) for announcements + resource linking
- Faculty monitoring but not actively participating daily (maintain profesional distance)

**Engagement Targets:**
- 50%+ student messages per week
- Response time: TA answers within 12 hours
- Monthly community highlights (best discussions, helpful peers)

### Peer Mentoring & Buddy System

**Buddy Pairing (First Week):**
- Match new students 1-1 based on:
  - Timezone (similar enough for overlap)
  - Career goals (finance buddy with finance peer, tech buddy with tech peer)
  - Experience level (experienced professional with career-switcher)
- Expected: Monthly check-ins, share project progress

**Peer Study Groups:**
- Students self-organize in Discord (e.g., "AWS Lab Study Group", "Python Pair Programming Tuesday 10am UTC")
- Faculty encourage (mention in announcements, provide room on Zoom)
- No faculty requirement to attend
- Peer-led = peer learning

**Teaching Assistants (Advanced Students):**
- Recruit strong performers from Cohort 1 to TA for Cohort 2
- Paid position (reduce workload, provide mentoring)
- TA duties: Office hours, forum moderation, lab troubleshooting, grading support

**MSBAi Alumni Association:**
- Organize monthly "Coffee Chats" (Zoom, casual 30-min reunions)
- Job board (exclusive to MSBAi alumni)
- Mentor match (alumni mentor current students on career questions)

1. **Resume Review:** 1-on-1 resume critique + portfolio guidance
2. **Interview Prep:** Mock interviews, behavioral prep, technical interview coaching
3. **Job Search Strategy:** Personalized pathway based on career goal
4. **LinkedIn Optimization:** Help craft compelling profile
5. **Salary Negotiation:** Coaching on offers + negotiation
6. **Employer Relationships:** Partner with companies for internships, hiring events

**Job Placement Target:** 70%+ placed within 6 months of graduation

**Affinity Groups** (Student-led, optional):

## Part 4: Global Time Zone Strategy

### Time Zone Accommodation Approach

**Rationale:**
- 30%+ international students expected
- No single time zone works for everyone
- Async-first philosophy enables flexibility

### Implementation

**Synchronous Session Timing:**

Three rotating time slots for Project Studio (repeat weekly):

| Slot | Time (UTC) | Covers |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **A** | Tue 10:00-11:30 | Americas (California morning, East Coast evening) |
| **B** | Wed 18:00-19:30 | Europe evening, West Africa morning, South America late night |
| **C** | Sat 14:00-15:30 | Australia/Asia morning (Thu evening in US = Sat morning in AU) |

**Student Choice:**
- At enrollment, choose preferred slot (A, B, or C)
- Can switch mid-semester with notice
- All sessions recorded for async access (no penalty for missing live)

**Office Hours:**
- Spread across 24 hours (at least 2-3 slots daily)
- Instructors work shift pattern (some early, some late)
- Discord/async for true async-first students

**Analytics Conversation (Guest Speakers):**
- Schedule at "middle" time that's reasonable for Americas + Europe (11:00-12:30 UTC)
- Record immediately for Asia/Pacific access
- Recorded Q&A available on forum (async participation)

### Time Zone Support Resources

**Documentation:**
- "What time is that in my timezone?" converter (link in every syllabus)
- Course calendar in each student's local timezone (Canvas integration)
- Monthly schedule announcement 2 weeks in advance

**Flexibility:**
- No "must be online at this time" requirement
- All major deadlines 11:59pm UTC (late enough for Americas, early enough for Australia)
- Catch-up sessions recorded + available on demand

## Part 5: Student Onboarding (First Week)

### Pre-Enrollment Preparation (Aug 1-24)

**Emails Sent:**
1. **Welcome email** + link to GitHub setup guide
2. **Tech check** + Zoom test video
3. **Buddy assignment** + intro from peer mentor
4. **Logistics** (Canvas access, Discord link, schedule)
5. **Mindset prep** ("You're joining a cohort of X amazing people")

### Onboarding Week (Week 0, before course launch)

**Synchronous Onboarding (Mandatory, live Zoom):**

**Monday (90 min):**
- Welcome + cohort introduction
- Program overview + expectations
- Community norms + code of conduct
- Tech troubleshooting (LIVE: test Jupyter, GitHub, AWS)

**Wednesday (60 min):**
- Meet the instructors + TA team
- Course structure walkthrough
- Project overview + first assignment
- Office hours + support resources

**Friday (60 min):**
- Buddy pair meet-up (15 min 1-on-1 with assigned buddy)
- Cohort social (optional, casual hangout on Discord)
- Final Q&A

**Asynchronous Content:**
- Pre-recorded tech setup videos (Python, Jupyter, GitHub, AWS)
- Course syllabi + expected workload
- "Success tips" from Cohort 1 graduates
- Mental health resources + counseling info

### First Assignment (Due by Sunday)

**Assignment:** Introduction post on Discord

**Prompt:** "Introduce yourself: What's your background? Why analytics? One fun fact?"

**Requirement:** Respond to 2 other students' intros

**Purpose:** Build community, normalize voice, show you're welcomed

## Part 6: Ongoing Engagement & Support

### Monthly Rhythms

**Week 1 of Course:** New content, project launches
**Week 2:** Studio sessions + first draft submissions
**Week 3:** Mid-week check-in, adjustment needed?
**Week 4:** Final projects due, transition to next course

**Monthly All-Hands (Cohort Meeting):**
- Last Friday of month (30-45 min)
- Celebrate wins (projects shipped, jobs landed, etc.)
- Address concerns + collect feedback
- Upcoming news + schedule adjustments
- Optional: Social hangout after

### Feedback & Iteration

**Mid-Course Survey (Week 4 each course):**
- "How's the pace? Too fast/slow/right?"
- "Studio sessions helpful?"
- "Any technology issues?"
- "Support from instructors/TAs: rate from 1-5"
- "How's your mental health + work/life balance?"
- **Response:** Share results, make adjustments in Week 5+

**End-of-Course Survey:**
- Same as mid-course + additional questions
- "Would you recommend to a friend? Why?"
- "What could we improve?"
- "Which instructor was most helpful? Why?"
- **Response:** Faculty review together, adjust for next cohort

**Advisory Board (Quarterly):**
- 8-10 student representatives from cohort (rotate roles)
- Meet with program director + faculty
- Discuss: What's working? What needs fixing?
- Propose changes for implementation next cohort

## Part 7: Student Wellness & Support

### Mental Health & Wellness

**Proactive Support:**
- Normalize stress ("This is hard; that's normal")
- Faculty acknowledge workload ("I know you're also working full-time")
- Optional "wellness breaks" (if course gets heavy, add deadline extension)
- Peer support circles (students help each other through difficult weeks)

**Crisis Resources:**
- Link to university counseling (free sessions for enrolled students)
- CARES office (financial hardship, food insecurity, housing)
- Crisis hotline contact (24/7 mental health support)
- Faculty training: How to refer student needing help

**Workload Monitoring:**
- Track late submissions (might signal struggle)
- Check in 1-on-1 if pattern emerges
- Offer flexibility ("Can we extend your deadline?")
- Connect to tutoring if academically stuck

### Financial Support

**Affordability:**
- Transparent tuition costs (no surprises)
- Payment plans (spread over 8 weeks)
- Scholarships + need-based aid (actively recruited underrepresented students)
- Open to employer sponsorship

**Subsidized Tools:**
- AWS credits provided (students don't pay out-of-pocket)
- Tableau Public free (no cost for learners)
- Free Jupyter environments (Google Colab, cloud-hosted)
- Textbook PDFs/open-source alternatives (no textbook costs)

## Part 10: Cohort Model Metrics & Success Criteria

### Community Health Metrics

| Metric | Target | How Measured |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| **Discord engagement** | 50%+ students post per week | Message count, unique posters |
| **Studio attendance** | 70%+ live attendance | Zoom attendance logs |
| **Office hours usage** | 60%+ students use at least once | Sign-up sheets |
| **Peer mentoring** | 80%+ have 1+ interaction with buddy | Buddy survey |
| **Course satisfaction** | NPS >70 | End-of-course survey |
| **Sense of belonging** | 75%+ feel part of cohort | Survey question: "I feel part of a community" |
| **Mental health** | Minimal crisis incidents | Refer to counseling office |
| **Retention** | 90%+ complete degree | Enrollment tracking |

### Engagement Interventions (If Metrics Slip)

**Low Discord Engagement:**
- Instructor highlights best discussions (feature student work)
- Gamification: "Helpful response of the week" + recognition
- Spark conversations (pose interesting questions)

**Low Studio Attendance:**
- Survey: "Why didn't you attend? Technical barrier? Timing? Motivation?"
- Adjust scheduling if timezone issue
- Show recordings are valuable (add transcripts + highlights)
- Celebrate those who attend (peer pressure, positive)

**Low Office Hours Usage:**
- Normalize asking for help ("Expert engineers pair program")
- Instructor offers 1-on-1 check-ins (proactive)
- TA-led group tutoring (safer than 1-on-1 for anxious students)
